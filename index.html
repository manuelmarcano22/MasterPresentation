Overview

      Objectives

      Our goal is to try to understand the formation of compact binaries when they are formed in a crowded and high density environment (like in a cluster of stars), and when you give them enough time to evolve and interact with other stars (like in a globular cluster).

      Compact Binaries

      Cataclysmic Variables

      • White dwarf as primary:
        • $0.3 \text{ M}_\odot \lesssim \text{M}_{\text{wd}} \lesssim 1.4 \text{ M}_\odot$
        $\rho_{\text{wd}} \approx 10^9$ kg/m$^3$
      • MS star companion
      • Accretion (usually) via a disk.
        • Disk instability: dwarf novae
        • Thermonuclear Runaway: classical Nova
      • 75 mins < $P_{orb}$ < 6 hrs

      Low-mass X-ray Binaries

      • Neutron star (or black hole) as primary:
        • $1 \text{ M}_\odot \lesssim \text{M}_{\text{wd}} \lesssim 2 \text{ M}_\odot$
        $\rho_{\text{wd}} \approx 10^{20}$ kg/m$^3$
      • MS star or WD companion
      • Accretion (usually) via a disk.
        • Unsteady accretion: Type II X-ray bursts
        • Thermonuclear Runaway: Type I X-ray bursts
      • More energetic than CVs

      Binaries in Globular Clusters

      • Globular Clusters
        • The binary population drives the dynamical evolution of GCs.
      • Cataclysmic Variables:
        • Potentially very different from field CVs.
        • A lot of open questions.
      • Neutron Stars:
        • Known distance binary in GC.
        • Estimate equation of state (mass-radius relation).
      • Gravitational Waves:.
        • Neutron stars binaries sources for LIGO.
        • White dwarf degenerate sources for eLISA

            Conclusions

            • Primordial CVs
              • 'Faint' undetected CVs H$\alpha$.
              • Primordial in the outer regions?
            • Periods:
              • Too short exposures (100 secs first night)
              • Secondary Mass - Period relation:
                • Big error bars mass ratio determination
                • No TiO and turnoff point: $0.5 \text{ M}_\odot \lesssim \text{M}_{\text{sec}} \lesssim 0.8 \text{ M}_\odot$
            • Magnetism:
              • No ionised helium (He II)
              • Strongest He II outside range (4686 Å)
            • Dwarf novae:.
              • Expected at least one (biased and empirical estimate)
              • Very rare in globular clusters. Suggest magnetic in nature

            Conclusions

            We have demonstrated how an IFU like MUSE can be used to efficiently study the population of compact objects in globular clusters. Deeper observation are needed but we exposed some hints that the population of field CVs and CVs in GCs are different.
